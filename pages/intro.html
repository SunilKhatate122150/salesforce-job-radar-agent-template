Self-Introduction — Your 2-Minute Story
The most important 2 minutes of the interview. This script is tailored for a Senior Salesforce Developer with US Mortgage experience.
Tell me about yourself — Master Script Memorize This First
Primary Script ( Pune / US Mortgage Focus)
"Thank you for this opportunity. My name is Sunil, and I'm a Salesforce Developer with four years of experience, currently based in Pune, Maharashtra. My expertise is concentrated in the US mortgage and financial services domain — which I believe makes me a strong fit for roles that involve complex, regulated business processes.

Professionally, I've worked both as a solo end-to-end developer and as a Senior Developer in team environments. In my most recent role at Inflooens India Operations, I was involved in building mortgage origination systems from the ground up — covering everything from custom Apex triggers and Lightning Web Components to third-party integrations with DocuSign for document generation, Experian for credit bureau pulls, and OFAC screening systems for regulatory compliance.

One of my most challenging and rewarding projects was a solo-built Loan Officer Campaign Platform where I integrated OpenAI GPT-4o for AI-driven content, the FRED API for economic data, and Freddie Mac's rate service — all within a single Salesforce org. That experience gave me deep confidence in end-to-end development and complex API architecture.

I'm now looking for a Senior Salesforce Developer role where I can bring this domain expertise and technical depth to help a team build scalable, compliant financial systems. I'm excited about this particular opportunity because..."
Practice Guidelines
  • Timing: 1:45 to 2:15 minutes. Not shorter (too shallow), not longer (rambling).
  • Eye Contact: Look directly at the camera, not your screen or notes.
  • Energy: Warm, professional, and slightly enthusiastic. You should sound like a colleague they WANT to work with.
  • The "Why Us": Always end with a specific reason why their company is different from others.
Variation: The Technical Deep-Dive Final Rounds

While many developers focus on basic configuration, my core strength lies in building complex, event-driven systems that stay well within governor limits.
