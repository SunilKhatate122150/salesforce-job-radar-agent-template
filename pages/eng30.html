30-Day English Speaking Plan — Rewritten
Day-by-day structured exercises. Follow this during your 8:00 AM communication block (30 min) and 5:00 PM STAR practice block (1 hr) every single day.
WEEK 1 — DAYS 1–7: FOUNDATION (Voice, Pace, Filler Words)
Day 1Record your baseline — 2-minute self-introduction on videoSit, press record, introduce yourself as if in a real interview with NO preparation. Watch it completely. Write down: every filler word (um, uh, basically, right, like, you know), your pace (rushed or slow?), whether you had a clear beginning and end. Keep this recording — you will compare it on Day 30. This is your starting point, not a judgment of where you will be.
Day 2Read tech article aloud at 70% normal speedOpen Salesforce Developer Blog. Read one complete article out loud — every word — at intentionally slow pace. Record 3 minutes. Play back: could someone who speaks slowly understand every word? The sentences you rushed through most — repeat those 5 times slowly. Slow speech sounds like confidence. Fast speech sounds like nervousness.
Day 3Filler word elimination — ONE DAY CHALLENGEFrom Day 1 recording: identify your top 2 filler words. Today: every time you feel the urge to say that word — STOP, take one breath, then say the sentence without it. Do this in ALL conversations today, not just practice. Silence before a sentence is confidence — not weakness. Track how many times you catch yourself.
Day 4Explain governor limits to an imaginary non-technical personStand up. Pretend someone who has never coded is in front of you. Explain what governor limits are using a real-world analogy ONLY. No technical terms allowed. Try: "Imagine a restaurant that can only serve 100 customers per day..." Record 90 seconds. Play back. Did it make sense without ANY Salesforce knowledge? Try again until it does. This trains clarity of thinking.
Day 5Answer 3 questions recorded — watch — redo each immediatelyQuestions: "What is bulkification?" "What is a trigger handler?" "What is @wire?" Record each. Watch immediately after each one — not all three at end. For each: did you start directly with the answer? Did you give a real project example? Did you end cleanly without trailing off? Redo every answer once with improvements applied. 6 total recordings today.
Day 6The power pause drill — 10 questions, 3-second pause before eachAsk yourself 10 questions from this guide aloud. After EACH question — deliberately pause and count 3 full seconds before starting your answer. This feels very uncomfortable. That is correct. In a real interview this 3-second pause tells the interviewer: "This person thinks before they speak." Do this 10 times until it starts feeling normal.
Day 7Self-introduction × 5 with different energy levelsRecord your self-introduction 5 times consecutively. Version 1: completely flat and monotone. Version 2: your normal way. Version 3: confident and direct. Version 4: enthusiastic. Version 5: warm, natural, like talking to a colleague you respect. Watch all 5. Which sounds like someone you would want on your team? That energy is your target. Practice that version 3 more times.
WEEK 2 — DAYS 8–14: STRUCTURE (PREP Framework, STAR, Clean Endings)
Day 8Learn PREP framework — practice with 5 technical answersPREP = Point → Reason → Example → Point again. For "What is bulkification?" — POINT: "Bulkification means writing Apex that processes multiple records at once within governor limits." REASON: "Salesforce can pass 200 records to a trigger at once — SOQL inside a loop hits the 100-query limit." EXAMPLE: "In my mortgage trigger, I collect all Account IDs in a Set, query once outside the loop, process from a Map." POINT AGAIN: "Rule: never put SOQL or DML inside a for loop." Practice 5 different answers using this structure today.
Day 9Project bridge drill — connect EVERY answer to your projectTake 5 technical questions. Answer with PREP. Then FORCE yourself to end every single answer with: "...and I used this exact pattern in my Loan Officer Campaign Platform when building the OpenAI integration" or "...this came up specifically in the Mortgage Origination Platform with the Experian callout." Answers connected to your real work score 3x higher in interviews than textbook answers.
Day 10STAR method — 2 complete stories timedOpen Behavioral section. Pick 2 STAR stories. Read once. Close guide. Tell the complete story out loud from memory — all 4 parts: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Set stopwatch. Target: 2 to 2.5 minutes each. Under 90 seconds: your Situation and Action need more detail. Over 3 minutes: trim the Action section — it is too long. Result MUST contain a number or measurable outcome.
Day 11Self-introduction × 10 variations — own every versionSay your self-introduction 10 times today. Each time change ONE element: what you open with, which project you highlight, which skill you emphasize, what domains you mention, how you close. By attempt 10, the introduction is so thoroughly practiced it sounds completely natural — not memorized — because you have practiced every variation. This is how professional speakers prepare.
Day 12"I don't know" recovery framework — 5 practice scenariosPick 5 topics from this guide you are genuinely less confident about. For each, practice this structure aloud: "That is a good area — I have not worked with [topic] directly in production. However, based on my experience with [related thing I know well], I would expect this to work by [reasoning]. I would verify this in the documentation before implementing." Never stop at "I don't know." Always bridge to what you DO know.
Day 13Vocabulary: 3 words × 10 sentences each spoken aloudPick 3 words from the Vocabulary section. For each word: make 10 different sentences using it in a Salesforce or interview context. Say all 10 sentences aloud for each word. 30 total spoken sentences. By the end of the day these 3 words must feel completely natural in your speaking — not like vocabulary words you are trying to use, but like words you actually use.
Day 14Week 2 recording — compare to Day 1Record: 2-minute self-intro + answer to "What is a trigger handler pattern?" + one full STAR story. Watch all three recordings. Now immediately watch your Day 1 recording. Write 5 specific improvements you can see and hear. Write 3 things still to work on. Seeing tangible progress is psychologically important — do not skip this comparison step.
WEEK 3 — DAYS 15–21: DEPTH (Senior English, Tradeoffs, Technical Clarity)
Day 15"Yes, and..." upgrade — make 5 short answers senior-levelTake 5 simple yes/no questions. Practice upgrading each from junior to senior. Junior: "Yes, I use with sharing." Senior: "Yes, I always declare sharing explicitly. I use with sharing for service classes that run in user context, without sharing for batch jobs needing unrestricted access, and inherited sharing for utility classes that should adapt to their caller. Being explicit about sharing intent makes code immediately readable to any reviewer." Practice 5 upgrades today.
Day 16"It depends — here is how I decide" — tradeoff thinkingSenior developers never give one absolute answer to comparison questions. Pick 5 comparison questions (wire vs imperative, Future vs Queueable, Flow vs Trigger). For each, practice: "It depends on the specific context. Here is how I decide..." then give a clear decision framework. An interviewer hearing "it depends + clear reasoning" gives you senior credit. "Always use X" without nuance suggests limited experience.
Day 17Architecture whiteboard explanation — 10 full minutes aloudTake paper. Draw the Mortgage Origination Platform architecture: objects, LWC components, integrations (Experian, DocuSign, OFAC, Platform Events, LOS sync). While drawing, explain every component aloud — what it is, why it exists, what it connects to, why you designed it that way. Do not stop. 10 full minutes of continuous spoken explanation. This is exactly what "walk me through your architecture" means in senior final rounds.
Day 1890-second timer drill — answer under time pressurePick 5 complex scenario questions. Set a phone timer for 90 seconds. Start answering. Stop when timer beeps — even mid-sentence. Play back: did you lead with the most important point? Did you get to an example? This trains you to front-load your highest-value information — critical when an interviewer interrupts or redirects you mid-answer.
Day 19Confidence phrase replacement — start answers professionallyToday start EVERY answer with one of these (rotate through them): "In my experience building mortgage systems..." / "What I have found in production is..." / "The approach I take is..." / "Let me give you a concrete example from my project..." Record 5 answers with these openers. Compare to answers starting with "So basically..." The difference in professional authority is immediately audible — even to yourself.
Day 20Handle challenges gracefully — 5 difficult scenariosPractice aloud: A) "Your answer was vague — be more specific." Respond: "You are right — let me be more precise: [add code, number, or project detail]." B) "That approach seems overcomplicated." Respond: "That is fair — the simpler approach would be X, but the tradeoff is Y. For our use case, the complexity was justified because..." C) "I disagree with that design." Respond: "I appreciate that perspective. Could you tell me what concern you have? That would help me address it specifically." Practice each 5 times.
Day 21Full 30-minute mock interview — Week 3 checkpointSet a 30-minute timer. Ask yourself 8 questions from different sections. For each: power pause → PREP structure → project connection → clean ending. Record all 30 minutes. Watch completely. Score yourself 1–5 on each: pace, filler words, structure, project examples, confidence, clean endings. Total target by Week 3: average 4.0 or higher on all dimensions.
WEEK 4 — DAYS 22–30: POLISH (Interview-Ready Performance)
Day 22Active listening drill — confirm before answeringFor every question today: before answering, say "Just to confirm — you are asking about [X] in the context of [Y]? Good. So..." Then answer. This demonstrates that you listened carefully, think before speaking, and answer the actual question not a related one you assumed. Practice with 10 questions. Most candidates skip this — it immediately signals senior communication maturity.
Day 23Professional closing — practice 5 times until completely naturalPractice this exact closing 5 times until it sounds like your own words: "Thank you — I genuinely enjoyed this conversation. Learning about [specific technical challenge or project they mentioned] has reinforced my excitement about this role. I have one question: based on what you have heard today, do you have any concerns about my fit that I can address? And what are the next steps?" The "concerns" question separates the most confident candidates from everyone else.
Day 24STAR story marathon — all 6 stories back to back from memoryTell ALL 6 STAR stories from the Behavioral section consecutively — no guide, no notes, from memory only. Each one 2–2.5 minutes. Total time approximately 15 minutes. Then pick the 2 that sounded weakest. Redo those 2 stories 3 more times each until they sound as strong as your best story. By Day 30 every story must be equally strong and natural.
Day 25Mortgage domain vocabulary in English — 5 concepts explained clearlyFor each of these terms: DTI, LTV, TRID, ECOA, OFAC — explain in clear confident English sentences: what it means, why it matters for mortgage lending, and how your Salesforce system handled it. Record 5 explanations. A domain-specialist interviewer at a mortgage company will ask about these — your domain knowledge must sound as natural as your Salesforce knowledge.
Day 26Weakness audit — targeted drilling on YOUR specific weak spotsReview your last 5 recordings. Identify the 3 weakest specific moments — a specific answer that still rambles, a specific technical term you still stumble over, a specific question type that still makes you rush. Spend today's ENTIRE communication practice on ONLY those 3 things. Targeted drilling on specific weaknesses is 10x more effective than general review at this stage.
Day 27Mirror practice — body language and voice togetherStand in front of a full-length mirror or phone camera facing you. Answer 5 questions while watching yourself. Notice: eye contact with yourself (practice looking at camera not down at notes), facial expression (engaged and warm, not flat or anxious), shoulder position (relaxed not raised with tension), and hand gestures (natural, supporting your words). Body language directly affects how your voice sounds. Relaxed body = more confident voice.
Day 28Speed variation — same answer at 3 different pacesPick one complex answer (your trigger handler explanation). Record it: Version 1 at your natural nervous speed. Version 2 at 20% slower. Version 3 at 40% slower than natural. Play all three back. Version 2 or 3 will sound most confident, clear, and authoritative. Version 1 will sound rushed. Train yourself to default to version 2 pace in all interviews.
Day 29Full dress rehearsal — 60 minutes, complete interview simulationSit at your desk. Dress appropriately — psychologically primes your brain for interview performance. Do the full 60-minute Mock Interview 3 from this guide. Record completely. Watch all of it without stopping. Write your honest assessment: what would make an interviewer hire you based on this recording, and what concerns would they still have. Fix those concerns — you have one day left.
Day 30Day 30 recording — compare to Day 1 — your transformationRecord the exact same scenario from Day 1: 2-minute self-intro + 3 technical Q&A answers. Watch this recording. Now immediately watch your Day 1 recording. The differences will be visible and audible: pace, clarity, structure, confidence, filler words. Write what you observe. This is 30 days of daily practice made visible and real. You are ready.
