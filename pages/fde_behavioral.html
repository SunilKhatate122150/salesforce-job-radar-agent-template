How would you explain Agentforce to a non-technical bank executive in 2 minutes?FDE▼
  • "Think of Agentforce as hiring a new team member who works 24/7, never takes a day off, and handles thousands of conversations simultaneously."
  • "When your borrower texts 'what is my balance?', this AI employee looks up their account and responds — in seconds, without a human involved."
  • "When the situation is complex or sensitive — like a rate dispute — they immediately hand it to your licensed loan officer with a full conversation summary."
  • "Because it's built on Salesforce, every conversation is logged, auditable, and secure — your compliance team has full visibility."
FDE skill

Translating technical concepts into business value. Practice until it sounds natural.

"Agentforce is just a chatbot, we tried chatbots and they failed." How do you respond?FDE▼
  • Acknowledge: "That's a fair concern — most chatbots failed because they were rule-based systems that broke when users said something unexpected."
  • Differentiate: "Agentforce doesn't follow a script. It understands intent, reasons through multi-step problems, and takes real actions."
  • Prove: "The key difference is grounding — it's not making up answers. It pulls YOUR actual customer data to answer accurately."
  • Trust: "The Einstein Trust Layer means your data never leaves Salesforce's secure infrastructure."
Pattern

Objection handling: empathy first → educate → reassure. Very common FDE interview pattern.

What are the real limitations of Agentforce you'd proactively share?FDE▼
  • LLM hallucination risk if grounding data is incomplete — mitigated but not eliminated
  • Complex reasoning limits — most scenarios handled, but extremely complex orchestration may need custom code
  • Token limits — long conversations or large grounding data can hit context window
  • Cost — LLM inference adds up at scale; model usage patterns before broad deployment
  • Regulatory — in mortgage/healthcare, certain decisions legally cannot be AI-made
  • Evolving tech — some features in Beta/Pilot; set roadmap expectations honestly
Trust builder

FDEs who share limitations proactively earn trust. It shows you're a partner, not a salesperson.

Why this matters

This tests FDE core skill: translating deep tech into business language. They want to see you do this naturally.

"Customer's POC isn't working after 2 weeks. They're frustrated. What do you do?"BEHAVIORAL▼
  • Listen: "Tell me specifically — wrong answers? Slow? Missing features?"
  • Diagnose: Check conversation logs → grounding data → prompt templates → action errors
  • Quick wins: Improve topic descriptions, add negative instructions, fix data quality
  • Set expectations: "Accuracy improves iteratively. First 2 weeks = learning phase."
  • Build confidence: Show improvement metrics over next sprint

"Do you have any questions for us?" — Your prepared list.FDE▼
Never do this

NEVER say "No, I'm good." Always ask at least 2 questions.

  • 1. "What does a typical first 90 days look like for an FDE Associate?"
  • 2. "How does the FDE team collaborate with Product Engineering when customers hit limitations?"
  • 3. "What's the most common challenge FDEs face in Agentforce deployments right now?"
  • 4. "How do you measure FDE success — customer outcomes, adoption, or something else?"
  • 5. "What Agentforce roadmap features are you most excited about?"
Best choice

Pick 2–3 based on conversation flow. #3 is strongest — shows you're already thinking about challenges.
