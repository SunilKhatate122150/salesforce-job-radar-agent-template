Communication Scripts — Difficult Moments
Battle-tested phrases for when the interview gets tough, vague, or critical.
When You Don't Know the Answer
  • "I haven't worked with that specific feature in a production environment yet. However, based on my deep experience with [Related Topic], I would expect the logic to follow [Reasoning]. I'd verify this in the documentation before implementation."
  • "That's an interesting edge case. To be honest, I'd need to research the specific governor limits for that scenario, but my initial approach would be [X] because of [Y]."
When Challenged on Your Architecture
  • "That's a valid point. In a massive-scale environment, I agree that [Their Approach] would be superior. In the specific context of my project, we chose [My Approach] because [Specific Reason: Time/Budget/Volume]."
  • "I appreciate that perspective. Could you share more about the specific challenge you've seen with this pattern? It would help me refine my thinking."
Clarifying Vague Questions
  • "Just to make sure I give you the most relevant answer — are you asking about the technical implementation or the high-level design strategy?"
  • "When you say [Term], are you referring to [Interpretation A] or [Interpretation B]? The approach differs significantly between the two."
Pro-Tip: The "Bridge" Technique
Always bridge from the theoretical to the practical.

"In theory, a Future method is used for X, and I actually implemented this in our mortgage platform when we needed to handle async document generation without blocking the UI."
