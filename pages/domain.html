Loan Lifecycle — Full Flow

1. Pre-Qualification

Borrower provides basic info (income, assets, debt). LO gives informal estimate of loan amount. No credit pull, no commitment. Quick assessment — usually same day.

2. Loan Application (1003)

Borrower completes Uniform Residential Loan Application (Form 1003). Credit is pulled here (hard inquiry). TRID clock starts — Loan Estimate must be delivered within 3 business days.

3. Loan Processing

Processor collects all documentation: W2s, pay stubs, bank statements, tax returns, title report, appraisal ordered. Verifies employment (VOE), assets (VOA), income calculations. Typically 1–2 weeks.

4. Underwriting

Underwriter analyzes the full loan file. Evaluates 3 Cs: Credit (FICO, history), Capacity (DTI, income), Collateral (appraisal, LTV). Issues Conditional Approval or Denial. Most complex stage.

5. Conditional Approval

UW approves with conditions: "Provide updated bank statement", "Explain large deposit", "HOA letter required". Processor works with borrower to satisfy conditions. Loan goes back to UW for final clear.

6. Clear to Close (CTC)

All conditions satisfied. UW gives Clear to Close. Closing Disclosure must be delivered 3 business days before closing date (TRID requirement). CD shows final loan terms.

7. Closing

Borrower signs documents at title company or notary. Typically includes: Note, Deed of Trust, Closing Disclosure. Funds not disbursed yet — right of rescission period for refinances (3 days).

8. Funding

Lender wires funds to title company. Loan is "funded" — money disbursed to seller. Deed recorded at county. Borrower gets keys. Loan is now a live mortgage.

9. Post-Close / Servicing

Loan may be sold to secondary market (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac). Servicing transferred. Monthly payments begin. Escrow account managed for taxes and insurance.

Critical Compliance Regulations

TRID (TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure)

Effective 2015. Combines Truth in Lending Act and Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act disclosures.

  • Loan Estimate (LE): Must deliver within 3 business days of application
  • Closing Disclosure (CD): Must deliver 3 business days before closing
  • Tolerance rules: certain fees cannot increase by more than 10% at closing vs LE
  • In Salesforce: Track LE sent date, CD sent date, calculate closing date restrictions automatically
ECOA (Equal Credit Opportunity Act)

Prohibits discrimination in credit decisions based on race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age.

  • Adverse Action Notice: Must be sent within 30 days of denial
  • Must state specific reasons for denial — "creditworthiness" not sufficient
  • HMDA data collection feeds into ECOA compliance monitoring
  • In Salesforce: Auto-generate adverse action letter when status = Denied
FCRA (Fair Credit Reporting Act)

Governs how credit information is used and protected.

  • Permissible Purpose: Credit can only be pulled with borrower's signed written authorization
  • Borrower has right to dispute inaccurate info
  • In Salesforce: Store credit pull authorization date, link to signed document
  • Experian, Equifax, TransUnion are CRAs covered by FCRA
HMDA (Home Mortgage Disclosure Act)

Requires lenders to collect and report data on home loan applications to CFPB.

  • LAR (Loan Application Register) filed annually
  • Data includes: race, ethnicity, gender, income, loan type, action taken
  • Purpose: detect discriminatory lending patterns
  • In Salesforce: Custom object or fields to capture all required HMDA data points automatically
BSA/AML + KYC

Bank Secrecy Act / Anti-Money Laundering. Know Your Customer.

  • OFAC Screening: Check borrower against SDN (Specially Designated Nationals) list before funding
  • SAR: Suspicious Activity Report — must file if suspicious transaction detected
  • KYC: Verify identity using government ID, SSN, address verification
  • In Salesforce: OFAC API integration — your Experian/OFAC work applies here
Key Loan Metrics

DTI (Debt-to-Income Ratio): Total monthly debt / gross monthly income. Conventional max: 43-50%. FHA max: 57%.

LTV (Loan-to-Value): Loan amount / appraised value. Conventional max: 80% (without PMI). FHA: 96.5%.

FICO Score: Conventional min: 620. FHA min: 580 (500 with 10% down). VA: 620 typical.

PMI: Private Mortgage Insurance required when LTV > 80% on conventional loans.
