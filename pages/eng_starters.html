50 Sentence Starters for Technical Interviews
Use 5 different starters each day. Read them aloud every morning. By Day 30 they will feel completely natural — not rehearsed.
Starting a Technical Answer
  • "The way I think about this is..."
  • "In my experience building mortgage systems, what I have found is..."
  • "Let me walk you through how this works in practice..."
  • "The key distinction here is..."
  • "To answer directly: [answer]. The reason is..."
  • "There are two parts to this. First..."
  • "This is something I have worked with closely — let me be specific..."
  • "The short answer is [X]. Let me explain the reasoning..."
  • "In production code, the pattern I use is..."
  • "Let me give you a concrete example from my project..."
Explaining a Tradeoff
  • "It depends on the specific situation. Here is how I decide..."
  • "Both approaches work, but the tradeoff is..."
  • "I would choose [X] when [condition], and [Y] when [other condition]..."
  • "The advantage of [X] is [A], but the cost is [B]..."
  • "In theory [X] is simpler, but in production what I have seen is..."
  • "If performance is the priority, then [X]. If maintainability is the priority, then [Y]."
  • "My preference is [X] because [technical reason], though [Y] works well when..."
  • "The answer depends on your data volume. Below [N] records, [X] works fine. Above that..."
  • "From an architecture standpoint, I would weigh..."
  • "I have used both in production. The difference I noticed in real usage was..."
Connecting to Your Project
  • "I used this exact pattern in my Mortgage Origination Platform when..."
  • "This came up specifically when I was building the Experian integration..."
  • "A real example from my work: when I built the Loan Officer Campaign Platform..."
  • "In our five-person team project, I was responsible for this part..."
  • "The first time I encountered this in production was..."
  • "I built this from scratch in my solo project — let me walk you through my decisions..."
  • "This is something I dealt with in the DocuSign integration specifically..."
  • "We had a similar challenge with OFAC screening, where..."
When You Need a Moment to Think
  • "That is a good scenario — let me think through the approach..."
  • "Give me just a moment — I want to make sure I cover the most important parts..."
  • "There are a few ways to approach this — let me think which fits best..."
  • "Just to confirm — you are asking about [X] in the context of [Y]? Good, so..."
  • "Let me think through this the way I would in a real design session..."
  • "I want to give you a precise answer rather than a quick one..."
Ending an Answer Strongly
  • "...That is the core principle, and it is something I apply in every trigger I write."
  • "...The result was [measurable outcome]. That approach has served us well."
  • "...Does that answer your question, or would you like me to go deeper on any part?"
  • "...That is how I approach it — I can show you the actual code if useful."
  • "...That is my experience with this specifically in the US mortgage domain."
  • "...In summary: [one sentence]. That is the decision I would make."
Handling a Difficult Question
  • "I have not used that specific feature directly, but based on [related concept]..."
  • "That is a fair challenge — let me address it directly..."
  • "You raise a good point. The concern would be [X], and I would address it by [Y]..."
  • "I want to be honest: I have more experience with [similar thing]. Let me reason through it..."
  • "That is a scenario I would want to test in sandbox first, but my initial thinking is..."
  • "I appreciate the question — here is what I know and where I would verify further..."
