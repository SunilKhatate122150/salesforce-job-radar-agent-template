What is Agentforce and how does it differ from Einstein Bots?CORE

Agentforce is an evolution from intent-based bots to **autonomous agents**. Unlike bots that follow strict "If-Then" trees, Agentforce uses the **Atlas Reasoning Engine** to determine which "Action" to take based on the user's goal.

  • Intent vs. Goal: Bots respond to pre-defined intents; Agents pursue high-level goals.
  • Actions: Agents can invoke Apex, Flows, and Prompt Templates autonomously.
  • Data: Agents use real-time Data Cloud data for grounding without training the model.
The 5 Components of an Agent: Agents, Topics, Actions, Atlas, Trust LayerCORE
  • Agent: The persona/role (e.g., "Service Agent"). Contains instructions and guardrails.
  • Topics: Logic groupings (e.g., "Manage Appointments"). Groups related actions together.
  • Actions: The "Doing" part — @InvocableMethod Apex, Flows, or Prompt Templates.
  • Atlas: The "Brain" (Reasoning Engine) that decides which topic/action to use.
  • Trust Layer: The "Shield" that ensures security, PII masking, and zero-retention.
What is "Grounding" and why is it the most important AI term?CORE

Grounding is the process of providing **real-time factual context** (from Salesforce records or Data Cloud) to the LLM so it can generate accurate, non-hallucinated responses.

Power Phrase

"Without grounding, an agent is just a generic chatbot. Grounding gives it the context of YOUR customer and YOUR business rules."

Explain the Trust Layer's "Zero Data Retention" policy.TRUST

Salesforce has contractual agreements with LLM providers (like OpenAI, Azure, Anthropic) where they agree **NOT** to store any data sent to them via the Trust Layer and **NOT** to use that data to train their models. This is critical for regulated industries like Mortgage.
